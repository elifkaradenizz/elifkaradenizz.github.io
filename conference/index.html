7th International Multidisciplinary Conference

2024

Presented & Published

In this study, natural language processing (NLP) techniques were applied to detect and analyze sexism and hate speech on social media platforms. The project focused on transforming unstructured textual data into meaningful insights through multiple preprocessing steps including tokenization, lemmatization, and stop-word removal using the NLTK library. To capture semantic relationships within the text, Word2Vec embeddings were utilized, allowing the model to better understand contextual meanings. In addition, data visualization techniques such as WordCloud were used to highlight frequently occurring terms and patterns. Various machine learning algorithms were implemented to classify and detect harmful content with a high level of accuracy. This work not only demonstrates technical implementation but also contributes to addressing real-world social issues through AI-driven solutions.
